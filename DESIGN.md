# Hermes Voice Design Notes

Visual direction: modern operator console with restrained vintage instrument
panel cues.

Color:

- Backgrounds use tinted black-brown and green-black neutrals, not pure black.
- Primary accent is warm amber/brass for active voice state and key actions.
- Cyan is secondary and sparse, reserved for links or remote/technical status.
- Red is direct and limited to error or muted-mic states.

Typography:

- Interface typography should be compact and precise.
- Labels are uppercase or small caps only where they act like instrument panel
  labels; body copy stays normal case.
- Avoid oversized type inside settings, cards, and controls.

Layout:

- Settings and chat panels use square-ish 8px radii, quiet hairline borders,
  and stable control dimensions.
- Avoid nested cards. Use section dividers and grouped fields instead.
- Bottom dock should feel like a control rail, not a floating pill toy.

Motion:

- Keep transitions short and subtle.
- No bounce, elastic, or attention-seeking decorative animation.
